IC MEMO NORTHWIND_AI · Series A 2026.05.18

We recommend leading the round at $12M on $60M pre, subject to two confirmations.

POSITION

Proceed to partner vote.

CONVICTION 0.68 · 7 OPEN RISKS
Thesis

Northwind sells an evidence-grounded research workspace to credit and equities desks. ARR reached $2.1M in April[01] against $0.5M a year prior, with 118% net retention across the eleven customers from its 2025 cohort[02]. Three enterprise customers are paying above $300K ACV[03]; the company has not lost a paying customer to a competitor.

Why now

The window is the same window Glean and Hebbia rode, one layer up the stack: domain-specific agents that cite their sources. Buy-side desks are funding seats out of research budgets that were already approved[04], which keeps the sales cycle inside one quarter. Founders are ex-Palantir Foundry and ex-Stripe Atlas — distribution should not be the gating risk.

What would change the recommendation

Two of the eleven paying customers are mid-evaluation against a horizontal competitor; loss of either would push top-3 concentration above 50%[05]. We have asked for a reference call with both CIOs before close[06]. Separately, the company is operating two product lines on fourteen engineers; we want a written commitment to wind down the consumer surface[07] by Q3.

Path to next round

$5.4M cash plus $12M new gives roughly 18 months at current burn[08]. To clear a credible Series B, ARR needs to reach $8M with top-3 concentration under 30% and at least two named Fortune-500 logos in production.
